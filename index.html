Death penalty popularity

Despite fewer executions, capital punishment remains popular in the US

By Matt Knapp

Despite an exponential trend downwards in the number of executions performed as a result of capital punishment laws in the United States, favorability for the law remains high.

According to the most recent data available from the Department of Justice, in 2013, 39 prisoners were executed; 46 fewer executions than there were in 2000 -- a 54 percen difference. This was the fewest executions since 2008, and the second fewest since 1994.

This steady trend downwards isn’t reflected in the popularity of the death penalty, though, as public favorability remains high for this controversial means of justice.

A recent Gallup poll, comparing opinions from 2000 to 2016, indicate that there’s only a 10% difference in favorability for the death penalty in that period of time, from 70% to 60%.

Public opinion of capital punishment became even more evident in the 2016 election, where Nebraska voters chose to reinstate the death penalty after the Nebraska unicameral decided to abolish it.

“[We will] make capital punishment work again in Nebraska to protect public safety,” said Nebraska Governor Pete Ricketts in a statement after the referendum results were announced.
